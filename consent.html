What HuCon is

Human in Conversation is a global civic listening infrastructure. Every conversation you contribute to becomes part of a permanent public record — a podcast episode, a research transcript, and an S4E field report that informs how cities understand the lives of the people inside them. This is not a private gathering. It is a contribution to a body of work. We want you to know that before you walk in.

Our commitment to you

You are anonymous by default. Your name will never appear without your explicit permission. You can withdraw your consent at any time. But please read what follows carefully — some things, by the nature of what HuCon is, cannot be undone once published.

Required to participate

The following three things are conditions of participation. HuCon cannot function as a listening infrastructure without them. If you are not comfortable with any of these, we would ask you not to attend — and we respect that choice completely.

Audio recording — permanent public archive Required
The conversation will be recorded and published as a HuCon podcast episode. This recording is part of HuCon's permanent public archive and remains available indefinitely. Your voice will be heard, though your name will not be used unless you choose otherwise below. Once an episode is published, the audio cannot be fully retracted.
Written transcript Required
Your contribution will be transcribed and cleaned for readability. Transcripts form part of HuCon's permanent research archive. They are stripped of identifying details before any use unless you choose to be named.
Use in S4E white papers and research reports Required
Your contribution — including quotes and paraphrased content — will be used in HuCon's published S4E field reports, white papers, and research outputs. This is the core purpose of HuCon as a listening infrastructure. Content is always anonymised by default. Your name will never appear without your explicit permission below.
Why these three are required: HuCon exists to surface the lived experience of people in cities and bring it into the rooms where decisions are made. The audio, transcript, and research report are not separate products — they are the same act of listening, in three forms. Without all three, HuCon cannot fulfil its purpose as a civic listening infrastructure.

Your right to withdraw
You may withdraw your consent at any time by writing to hello@humansinconversation.com with your consent reference number. We will remove your contribution from any unpublished material within 14 days. Please note: because the HuCon podcast is a permanent public archive, audio that has already been published cannot be fully retracted — but we will remove or anonymise your contribution in all future uses and in any material not yet in the public domain. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

GDPR Privacy Notice — EU Regulation 2016/679
Data controller: Human in Conversation (HuCon), Italy. Contact: hello@humansinconversation.com

What we collect: Your name, email address, and the audio recording and written transcript of your contribution to the HuCon conversation.

Why we collect it: To fulfil HuCon's purpose as a civic listening infrastructure — producing podcast episodes, transcripts, and S4E field reports that inform how cities understand the lives of the people inside them. With your additional permission, to use selected quotes in public communications.

Legal basis: Your explicit, informed consent given through this form (Article 6(1)(a), GDPR). For the podcast and research archive, processing is also carried out in the public interest (Article 6(1)(e)).

Retention: Published podcast audio is retained indefinitely as part of HuCon's permanent public archive. Transcripts and anonymised research content are retained indefinitely. Your personal data (name and email) is retained for 3 years from the date of this consent, after which it is securely deleted.

Your rights: You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data. You may withdraw consent, restrict processing, or object to processing at any time. You may also lodge a complaint with Italy's data protection authority: Garante per la Protezione dei Dati Personali — garanteprivacy.it.

Data sharing: Your personal data is never sold or shared with third parties. Anonymised contributions appear in published research outputs and the public podcast as described above. HuCon may share anonymised, aggregated findings with city governments, research institutions, and funders.
