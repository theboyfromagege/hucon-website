What is HuCon

The stories
cities need
to hear.

HuCon — Humans in Conversation — is a global movement that creates intimate, guided listening tables in cities around the world. Each table begins with one question that truly matters to the people in the room.

Participants share honest, often anonymous stories about how they're really doing. Everything is audio-recorded and published on the HuCon Spotify archive — a living document of urban human experience.

Ultimately, these stories help cities, researchers, and AI designers build more human messages and interventions in an era when authentic human data matters more than ever.

How it works

Five steps.
One honest evening.

01

Pick a date & question

Choose a theme rooted in your city's reality — reinvention, identity, belonging, pressure. The question should matter to everyone at the table.

02

Find your tribe

Invite 3 to 10 people who agree to share their stories. It's a conversation, not a panel. Find people who'll be honest.

03

Choose a safe space

Somewhere guests can speak freely. A home, a quiet room, a trusted community space. You guide — that's all.

04

Press record

Audio only. No cameras. Ever. Participants may choose to share anonymously. Record and send to the global archive.

05

Share food & send

Bring a dish meaningful to your community. Tell the global audience its significance. Send your stories. You've hosted a HuCon.

The ritual

"Every table begins with food that means something to the community."

The HuCon bowl sits at the centre of every gathering. Sharing food is not incidental — it's the ritual that opens people to honesty. It signals: this is a different kind of conversation.

When you host a table, you choose a dish from your world, bring it to the room, and explain its meaning — to the people present, and to the global archive.

The mission

Cities that listen
design better lives.

In a world shaped by curated feeds and performance metrics, people have fewer spaces to speak honestly about reinvention, loneliness, belonging, and the real cost of change.

HuCon creates that space — and then sends what it hears back to the cities, researchers, and AI systems that need to understand what humans actually experience.
